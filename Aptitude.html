Profit and Loss

Formulas:

Profit and Loss Formulas

Important Facts:

  • Cost Price (C.P.): The price at which an article is purchased.
  • Selling Price (S.P.): The price at which an article is sold.
  • Profit or Gain: If S.P. is greater than C.P., the seller is said to have a profit or gain.
  • Loss: If S.P. is less than C.P., the seller is said to have incurred a loss.

Important Formulae:

  • Gain: Gain = (S.P.) - (C.P.)
  • Loss: Loss = (C.P.) - (S.P.)
  • Gain Percentage: Gain % = (Gain × 100) / C.P.
  • Loss Percentage: Loss % = (Loss × 100) / C.P.
  • Selling Price (S.P.) when Gain % is given: S.P. = [(100 + Gain %) / 100] × C.P.
  • Selling Price (S.P.) when Loss % is given: S.P. = [(100 - Loss %) / 100] × C.P.
  • Cost Price (C.P.) when Gain % is given: C.P. = [100 / (100 + Gain %)] × S.P.
  • Cost Price (C.P.) when Loss % is given: C.P. = [100 / (100 - Loss %)] × S.P.
  • If an article is sold at a gain of 35%: S.P. = 135% of C.P.
  • If an article is sold at a loss of 35%: S.P. = 65% of C.P.
  • Loss when two similar items are sold at a gain of x% and a loss of x%: Loss % = (x² / 100)%
  • Gain % when false weights are used: Gain % = (Error / (True Value - Error)) × 100%
